Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how PalaviTech ("BytesCop", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, shares, and protects information when you use the BytesCop platform, website, and related services (collectively, the "Service"). It applies to all users, including visitors who interact with our public pages and contact forms.

1. Key Definitions

  • "Personal Data" means information that can identify an individual directly or indirectly, such as name, email address, IP address, device identifiers, and account activity logs.
  • "Customer Content" means security findings, evidence artifacts, notes, attachments, reports, and other security-related data you upload to the Service.
  • "Data Controller" means the party that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data.
  • "Data Processor" means the party that processes Personal Data on behalf of a Data Controller.

2. Our Role: Controller vs. Processor

BytesCop acts as a Data Controller for account data (your name, email, login activity, preferences) and visitor data (contact form submissions). BytesCop acts as a Data Processor for Customer Content that you upload and manage within your Tenant. As a processor, we process Customer Content only on your instructions and as necessary to provide the Service.

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Information you provide

  • Account information: name, email address, organization name, password (stored as a cryptographic hash), and authentication settings (MFA configuration).
  • Customer Content: security findings, evidence, screenshots, attachments, and supporting artifacts you upload.
  • Contact form submissions: name, email address, organization (optional), subject (optional), and message content submitted via our public contact form. We also capture the submitter's IP address for abuse prevention.
  • Support communications: correspondence and feedback you send to us.

3.2 Information collected automatically

  • Device and connection data: IP address, browser type and version, and operating system, as captured in audit logs and server request logs.
  • Audit logs: detailed records of user actions within the Service, including sign-in attempts, administrative actions, data modifications, and the associated IP address, user agent, and timestamp. Audit logs are retained for security, compliance, and accountability purposes.

3.3 Session cookies and local storage

The Service uses secure, HTTP-only session cookies for authentication and session management. These cookies are strictly necessary for the Service to function, cannot be opted out of while using the Service, and are not accessible to client-side JavaScript. A separate cookie is used for CSRF protection. The Service may also use your browser's local storage for non-sensitive user interface preferences. No third-party tracking cookies are used. If we introduce optional cookies in the future (for analytics or preferences), we will implement appropriate consent mechanisms.

4. How We Use Information

We use information for the following purposes:

  • To provide, operate, maintain, and improve the Service.
  • To authenticate users, manage sessions, and enforce access controls.
  • To prevent fraud, detect abuse, enforce rate limits, and secure the Service.
  • To respond to inquiries, provide support, and communicate service-related notices.
  • To generate reports and enable features using Customer Content you upload.
  • To maintain audit logs for security, compliance, and accountability.
  • To generate anonymized, aggregated analytics for product improvement, if introduced in the future.
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.

5. Email Communications

We send the following types of emails, all of which are transactional and necessary for the operation of the Service:

  • Account emails: email verification, password reset, MFA enrollment/changes, and account security notifications.
  • Membership emails: team invitations and role notifications.
  • Tenant emails: data export notifications, account closure confirmations, and administrative alerts.
  • Contact form acknowledgments: confirmation that your inquiry was received.

These are not marketing communications and cannot be opted out of while maintaining an active account, as they are essential to the security and operation of the Service. We do not send marketing emails. If we introduce marketing communications in the future, they will require your explicit opt-in consent.

6. Legal Bases for Processing

Depending on your jurisdiction, we process Personal Data based on:

  • Contract performance: processing necessary to provide the Service (account management, authentication, feature delivery).
  • Legitimate interests: security monitoring, fraud prevention, audit logging, Service improvement, and abuse prevention.
  • Legal compliance: processing required by applicable laws (tax records, law enforcement requests, regulatory obligations).
  • Consent: where required by applicable law, such as for optional analytics or future marketing communications. You may withdraw consent at any time.

7. How We Share Information

We do not sell Personal Data. We may share information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Infrastructure providers (sub-processors): we use Amazon Web Services (AWS) for cloud hosting, storage (S3), email delivery (SES), message queuing (SQS/SNS), compute (EC2, Lambda), database (RDS), and content delivery (CloudFront). These services process data only as necessary to operate the Service.
  • Legal and safety: if required by law, subpoena, court order, or to protect the rights, safety, and security of our users, the public, or the Service.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, subject to confidentiality protections. We will notify you before your Personal Data is transferred to a new entity.
  • With your direction: when you choose to enable integrations, share reports, or export data to third-party services.

A current list of sub-processors is available upon request at privacy@bytescop.com. We will notify you of material changes to our sub-processor list.

8. Customer Content and Sensitive Data

The Service stores security data that may be sensitive or confidential. You are responsible for ensuring you have the right and authority to upload and process Customer Content. You must not upload regulated data (such as certain government identifiers, financial data subject to PCI-DSS, or health data subject to HIPAA) unless you have implemented all required safeguards and have obtained appropriate authorization. BytesCop does not access or review Customer Content except as necessary to provide the Service, respond to support requests, or address security incidents.

9. Data Processing Locations

Your data is primarily processed and stored in Amazon Web Services' Asia Pacific (Mumbai) region (ap-south-1), located in India. Email delivery is processed through AWS SES, which may route through AWS infrastructure in the same or nearby regions. If we expand to additional regions in the future, we will update this policy and use appropriate safeguards for international data transfers, such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or equivalent mechanisms recognized under applicable data protection laws.

10. International Data Transfers

If you access the Service from outside India, your information may be transferred to and processed in India. Where required by applicable law (such as the GDPR for transfers outside the EEA), we rely on appropriate transfer mechanisms including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and adequacy decisions. You may request a copy of the applicable transfer safeguards by contacting us at privacy@bytescop.com.

11. Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures to protect your information, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS) and at rest (AES-256 for exports and stored attachments).
  • Role-based access controls and tenant isolation.
  • Multi-factor authentication support.
  • Audit logging of administrative actions and data access.
  • Rate limiting and abuse prevention on all endpoints.
  • Regular security reviews and infrastructure hardening.

No system is 100% secure. You are responsible for maintaining strong credentials, enabling MFA, enforcing least-privilege access within your Tenant, and safely handling exported data and reports.

12. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as necessary to provide the Service, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Specific retention periods:

  • Account data: retained while your account is active. Upon Tenant closure, account data is permanently purged as part of the closure process.
  • Customer Content: retained until you delete it or close your Tenant. Upon Tenant closure, data is permanently and irreversibly purged after the closure confirmation process.
  • Audit logs: retained for a reasonable period for security and compliance purposes.
  • Rate limit records: automatically expire after 24 hours.
  • Background job records: automatically expire based on configured TTL (typically 30 days).
  • Contact form submissions: retained in email only (not stored in the Service database). Subject to our email provider's retention policies.

You may delete individual records (findings, evidence, clients) through the Service interface at any time. To permanently delete all data, use the Tenant closure feature in your account settings.

13. Breach Notification

In the event of a security breach that results in unauthorized access to, or disclosure of, Personal Data, we will (a) investigate the incident promptly, (b) take reasonable steps to contain and mitigate the breach, (c) notify affected users without undue delay, and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach where required by applicable law (such as GDPR Article 33), and (d) notify the relevant supervisory authority where required. Breach notifications will include the nature of the breach, the data involved, the measures taken, and contact information for further inquiries.

14. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your Personal Data:

  • Access: request a copy of the Personal Data we hold about you.
  • Correction: request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Deletion: request deletion of your Personal Data, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Portability: receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (available via the Service's data export feature).
  • Restriction: request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Objection: object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

For GDPR (EU/EEA) users:

You have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority if you believe your rights have been violated. A list of EU supervisory authorities is available at the European Data Protection Board website.

For CCPA (California) users:

  • Right to know: you may request details about the categories and specific pieces of Personal Data we collect, use, and share.
  • Right to delete: you may request deletion of your Personal Data, subject to applicable exceptions.
  • Right to opt-out of sale: we do not sell Personal Data. No opt-out is necessary, but we state this explicitly for transparency.
  • Non-discrimination: we will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights.

For DPDPA (India) users:

Under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you have the right to access, correct, and erase your Personal Data. You also have the right to nominate another person to exercise your rights in case of death or incapacity. We process your data based on your consent or for legitimate uses as defined under the DPDPA. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@bytescop.com.

To exercise any of the above rights, contact us at privacy@bytescop.com. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law (typically 30 days). We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

15. Automated Decision-Making

We do not use automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects you. Rate limiting and abuse prevention mechanisms operate automatically but are designed to protect the Service and its users, not to make decisions about individuals. If we introduce automated decision-making in the future, we will update this policy and provide appropriate safeguards, including the right to human review.

16. Children's Privacy

The Service is intended for business and professional use and is not directed to children under the age of 18 (or the applicable age of majority in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect Personal Data from children. If we become aware that we have collected data from a child, we will take steps to delete it promptly. If you believe a child has provided us with Personal Data, please contact us at privacy@bytescop.com.
